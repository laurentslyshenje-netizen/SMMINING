SM Mining Africa supports a broad range of major cryptocurrencies across deposits, investment plans, and withdrawals. Whether you are funding a new investment plan or moving profits to an external wallet, you can transact in the asset that suits you best. This page lists all supported assets and their key details.

<Warning>
  Always double-check your wallet address before sending funds. Cryptocurrency transactions are irreversible — SM Mining Africa cannot recover assets sent to an incorrect address.
</Warning>

## Using Crypto for Deposits

To fund your account with cryptocurrency:

1. Log in and go to **Dashboard > Wallet > Deposit**.
2. Select your asset from the list and copy the wallet address displayed.
3. Open your external exchange or wallet and send the desired amount to that address.
4. Wait for the required number of blockchain confirmations — your balance updates automatically once the transaction is confirmed.

Confirmation times vary by network. Bitcoin typically requires 2–3 confirmations, while assets like Litecoin and Dash confirm more quickly.

## Using Crypto for Withdrawals

To withdraw funds to an external wallet:

1. Navigate to **Dashboard > Wallet > Withdraw**.
2. Select the asset you want to withdraw.
3. Paste your external wallet address into the address field.
4. Enter the amount you want to withdraw, review the network fee, and click **Submit**.

Your withdrawal enters the processing queue and is broadcast to the blockchain once approved.

<Note>
  Minimum deposit and withdrawal amounts vary by asset. Check the **Wallet** section of your dashboard for current minimums before initiating a transaction.
</Note>

## Exchange Rates

All SM Mining Africa investment plans and account balances are denominated in USD equivalent. When you deposit a cryptocurrency, the platform converts its value to USD at the current market rate at the exact moment your transaction is confirmed on the blockchain. Withdrawal amounts are converted back to your chosen asset at the prevailing rate when the withdrawal is processed.
